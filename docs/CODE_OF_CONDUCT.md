# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Be respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accept constructive criticism
- Focus on what is best for the community
- Show empathy towards other community members
- Celebrate the achievements of others
- Help newcomers get started in the community

### Unacceptable Behavior

- The use of sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

- Insults, put-downs, or jokes that are offensive or exclusionary
- Sexist, racist, or otherwise discriminatory jokes or language
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Continued harassment after being asked to stop
- Posting private information about others without consent
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior

## Our Responsibilities

### Project Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Project maintainainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, or to ban temporarily or permanently any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Community Members

Community members are responsible for:
- Reading and understanding this Code of Conduct
- Treating all community members with respect
- Reporting violations to the appropriate maintainers
- Cooperating with investigations into violations
- Accepting feedback and adjusting behavior when needed

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of our community spaces include:

- GitHub repositories
- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Project documentation and wikis
- Official communication channels (Discord, Slack, email lists)
- Project-related events and conferences

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, please report it to the project maintainers.

- GitHub: [To be added]
- Discord: [To be added]

**What to Include:**
- Your contact information
- The nature of the violation
- Specific details (time, location, individuals involved)
- Any available evidence (screenshots, logs, etc.)

**Confidentiality:**
All reports will be kept confidential to the extent possible, and we will protect the identity of the reporter.

### Investigation Process

When a report is received:

1. The maintainers will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
2. An investigation will be conducted
3. The maintainers will determine appropriate action
4. The reporter will be informed of the outcome

### Possible Actions

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate, including but not limited to:

- A private warning
- A request for a public or private apology
- A temporary or permanent ban from the community
- Removal of content (comments, commits, etc.)
- Reporting to law enforcement (if necessary)

### Appeal Process

If you believe you have been unfairly accused or sanctioned:

1. Submit an appeal to the maintainers
2. Provide evidence and context
3. The maintainers will review within 7 days
4. A final decision will be made and communicated

## Additional Guidelines

### Communication Style

- Be constructive and helpful
- Assume good intentions
- Give credit where due
- Be patient with newcomers
- Focus on what is best for the community

### Inclusive Language

- Use gender-neutral language when possible
- Avoid cultural references that may not be universally understood
- Be mindful of time zone differences when scheduling meetings
- Respect different communication styles and preferences

### Conflict Resolution

If you have a conflict with another community member:

1. Try to resolve it privately first
2. If unresolved, contact a maintainer
3. Provide specific details and context
4. Be open to mediation
5. Accept the final decision

### Accessibility

We strive to make our community accessible to everyone:

- Use clear and simple language
- Provide alternative formats when requested
- Be patient with those who communicate differently
- Accommodate different time zones for meetings
- Provide advance notice for events
